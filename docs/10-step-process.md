# 10-Step Build Process

## 1. Product Foundation

Define the target users, core workflows, MVP scope, data model, and first release boundaries. Keep the product similar in category to MaintainX-style CMMS software, but build an original app with its own language, information architecture, and visual design.

## 2. Technical Foundation

Choose the stack, repo structure, auth approach, database, local development flow, deployment target, and quality gates. Produce a working app shell with navigation and seeded demo data.

## 3. Work Orders MVP

Build the central work order workflow: create, assign, prioritize, schedule, update status, add comments, complete, and view history.

## 4. Assets And Locations

Add asset and location records, QR-friendly asset identifiers, maintenance history, manuals/files metadata, and asset-to-work-order relationships.

## 5. Procedures And Inspections

Create reusable checklist templates, procedure steps, pass/fail readings, required photos/notes, and generated follow-up corrective work orders.

## 6. Preventive Maintenance

Add recurring schedules by time, meter reading, or asset condition. Generate planned work orders and show compliance against due dates.

## 7. Parts Inventory

Track parts, stock levels, suppliers, low-stock alerts, and parts consumed on work orders.

## 8. Technician Mobile Experience

Optimize the technician flow for phones and tablets: fast queue, offline-ready work packets, photo capture, voice/text notes, and simple completion.

## 9. Reporting And Operations Dashboard

Build dashboards for overdue work, completion rates, downtime, asset reliability, preventive compliance, labor time, and parts cost.

## 10. Collaboration, Integrations, And Hardening

Add team messaging around work orders, role permissions, audit logs, imports/exports, API/webhooks, ERP hooks, backups, monitoring, and production security hardening.
